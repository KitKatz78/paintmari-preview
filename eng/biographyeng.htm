Mari Ochi Moosreiner
Biography

Born in Kyoto, Japan, Mari Ochi Moosreiner, an artist and poet, lives and works in the U.S. and Japan. Her works are in private collections in Japan and the U.S. and have been exhibited, among others, at the New York Law School and Winkleman Gallery in New York, NY, AVA Gallery in Lebanon, NH, and Anne McKnight Gallery in Lisbon, NH. She has also participated in "The Seed Project" for Artworld Digest magazine in NY and on-line exhibitions at Organization of Independent Artists and Artists Space in NY. "The Book Project" is her open-ended project.

Moosreiner studied painting with Clifford West (an expressionist painter, filmmaker—and a friend of Milton Avery and Yasuo Kuniyoshi) and Shinichiro Nakamura, an impressionist portrait painter in Tokyo. She attended Doshisha University in Kyoto and has a B.A. in English from Tarkio College in Missouri where she received a scholarship for her senior year. After studying at Des Moines Art Center, she was awarded a Graduate Fellowship at Drake University in Des Moines and has an M.A. in English.

Her paintings, expressionistic and internal, are the products of her poetic sense and Japanese aesthetic sensibility deep-rooted in universal aesthetics. Her poetry awards, among her published poems in Japan and the U.S., include first prizes from the Tokyo English Literature Society and the Rhode Island State Poetry Society. With her graduate work at Drake, she formed a basis for her aesthetic philosophy as an artist. (Major influences from her writings at Drake are Milton, Keats, D.H. Lawrence, and Linguistics studies—her M.A. thesis was "Milton's Emerging View of Fame in Paradise Lost, Paradise Regained, and Samson Agonistes." Her father, Fumio Ochi, who inspired her artistic and literary path, was a prominent Milton scholar in Japan.)

She is a member of Organization of Independent Artists and Women's Caucus for Art.
