Mari Ochi Moosreiner
Statement

For years, I had pushed figurative images (which consequently merged into abstraction) to the limits of metaphor and language that defy definition. I have continued this search with abstract forms, while, on occasion, I explore images of self and identifiable images, objects, symbols, and letters that keep occurring in my inner consciousness and often have poetic relevance. Working in this way puts me in a flux for renewed search.

"Leda and the Swan," a poem by W.B. Yeats, one of my recurring preoccupations because of the poem's intense photographic image and its depth and universality; a "Bittersweet" series of works, with letters and words prominently incorporated as pictures, in which, stemming from indications of existentialism in images from movies depicting life as bittersweet, I extended the paradox and irony of bitterness (taste, harshness, void) and sweetness to my own aesthetic milieu—these are some of the part of my search.

Along the way, my focus is to find order in chaos, chaos in order—to create poetic language through paint surface. Understatement (unfinished brush strokes, withheld images) evokes depth of meanings. Self-transcendence (not resorting to self-indulgent sentimentalism) enables expressions beyond pretense and appearance. Texture and imagery emerge as lines, forms, and colors define and find their space; these mingle, intermingle, recede, disappear, but re-emerge again; define the non-defining, un-define the defined; a weaving of doing and undoing.

Creative action and processes urge me into the core of my being, beyond my existence, with freedom, infinite possibilities, and a transcendental glimpse of mystery, eternity, truth, beauty, intensity, or Zen nothingness; and I let my unconscious work its way out from the core, the essence.
